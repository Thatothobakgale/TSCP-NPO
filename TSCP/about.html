Meet The Founder Of TSCP

Tony Gloria Bodibe

Project origin

Tsogang Sechaba, meaning "WAKE UP AFRICA", was founded in 1999 by Tony Gloria Bodibe. The organization officially registered with Department of Social Development (DSD) on 16 October 2002, enabling access to funding, resources, and partnership. Since then, Tsogang Sechaba Community Project has continued to grow, responding to community needs and escalating its commitment to providing comprehensive support services.

Our Story

The Tsogang Sechaba Community Project is a dedicated organization committed to empowering vulnerable communities affected by HIV, STDs, and gender-based violence. Through a supportive network and comprehensive psychosocial services, we aim to foster resilience, ensure holistic well-being, and promote social justice among children, young adults, youth, and orphaned vulnerable children. Our mission is grounded in the belief that every individual deserves accessible and sustainable care to lead a dignified life. We strive to create a society where everyone has the support they need to thrive, free from the impacts of these social challenges.


Mission and Vision

Our mission

Our mission is to empower and support vulnerable communities affected by HIV, STDs, and gender-based violence through comprehensive psychosocial services, ensuring holistic well-being and fostering resilience among children, young adults, youth, and orphaned vulnerable children. We strive to provide accessible and sustainable care that promotes health, dignity, and social justice.

Our vision

A society where every individual, regardless of their circumstances, can access the support they need to live healthy, empowered lives free from the impacts of HIV, STDs, and gender-based violence.

Objectives

HIV and STD Prevention and Care:

  • Provide education and awareness on HIV and STD prevention.
  • Facilitate access to testing, treatment, and ongoing care for individuals living with HIV and STDs.
  • Offer nutritional support to improve the health outcomes of affected individuals.

    • Gender-Based Violence (GBV) Intervention:

  • Deliver immediate support and counseling services to survivors of gender-based violence.
  • Collaborate with the South African Police Service (SAPS) to provide intervention and for cases of substance abuse and GBV.
  • Promote community awareness and advocacy to reduce the prevalence of GBV.

    • Psychosocial Support for Vulnerable Population:

  • Offer counseling and emotional support through qualified social workers and social auxiliary workers.
  • Provide psychosocial support tailored to the needs of children, young adults, youth, and orphaned vulnerable children affected by HIV/GBV.
  • Conduct home visits by trained caregivers to assess and respond to the needs of household beneficiaries in hostels, informal settlements, and townships.

    • Nutritional Support:

  • Ensure the provision of balanced and adequate nutrition to improve the overall wellbeing of those affected by HIV and GBV.
  • Work with local and national partners to secure resources for sustainable nutritional programs.

    • Collaboration and Partnerships:

  • Strengthen partnerships with SAPS, healthcare providers, and other NGOs to ensure a coordinated response to HIV, STDs, GBV, and other related challenges.
  • Facilitate referrals and linkages to ensure that beneficiaries receive comprehensive care and support

    • Caregiver Support and Training:

  • Provide specialized training and ongoing support for older persons who serve as caregivers for children living with HIV, orphaned due to HIV, or affected by GBV and substance abuse.
  • Equip caregivers with the skills needed to manage the health and emotional wellbeing of these children, ensuring they can offer effective guidance and care.

    • Health and Wellness Initiatives:

  • Organize regular health screenings, exercise sessions, and wellness workshops tailored to older persons.
  • Promote healthy aging through nutritional guidance and activities that maintain physical and mental health, enabling caregivers to remain active and capable.

    • Social and Emotional Support Groups:

  • Facilitate support groups where older persons can share experiences, receive emotional support, and learn from each other.
  • Address the challenges of caregiving and provide a platform for discussing strategies to handle the unique needs of children affected by HIV, GBV, and substance abuse.

    • Victim Empowerment Programme:

  • Provide holistic support to individuals affected by crime, abuse, and violence.
  • Empower victims through counseling, legal assistance, and trauma interventions to regain control of their lives.
  • Foster resilience and recovery by offering safe spaces, psychosocial support, and access to critical resources.
  • Collaborate with law enforcement and community organizations to ensure comprehensive care and protection.
  • Restore dignity and facilitate healing for all victims through dedicated support services.
